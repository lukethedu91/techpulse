The End of the Password Era: Passkeys Just Hit Critical Mass

Apple, Google, and Microsoft have quietly made passwords optional for over 90% of consumer accounts. The credential-theft industry just had its foundation knocked out. Here's what that actually means.

By Staff Writer  ·   ·  8 min read

The transition has been slower than anyone hoped, but 2024 and 2025 quietly crossed thresholds that make the old username-and-password model look genuinely obsolete. Passkeys — cryptographic credentials tied to your device's biometrics — now work seamlessly across every major OS. The phishing industry is, for the first time, facing a threat it can't easily adapt around.
